# MyDeepTalk Wellbeing Framework (MWF)

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Foundational  
**Owner:** MyDeepTalk Product Team

---

# Overview

The MyDeepTalk Wellbeing Framework (MWF) is the core model that powers every experience within MyDeepTalk.

Rather than viewing wellbeing through the lens of symptoms or diagnoses alone, the framework views wellbeing as a continuous journey of self-awareness, healing, growth, and flourishing.

The framework provides a common language that connects:

- Assessments
- Genesis Journeys
- Journaling
- Therapist Care
- Homework
- Artificial Intelligence
- Progress Tracking
- Recommendations

Every feature within MyDeepTalk should align with this framework.

---

# Guiding Principles

## Person Before Diagnosis

People are more than diagnoses, symptoms, or assessment scores.

Every interaction should seek to understand the whole person.

---

## Healing Is A Journey

Healing is rarely linear.

People grow through reflection, setbacks, learning, relationships, and intentional action.

The platform supports this ongoing journey rather than promising instant transformation.

---

## Awareness Comes Before Change

Transformation begins with awareness.

Before people can change their behaviours, they must understand themselves.

Every MyDeepTalk experience should increase awareness.

---

## Growth Is Continuous

Wellbeing is not binary.

Nobody is permanently "healthy" or "unhealthy."

People continuously grow throughout life.

---

## Professional Care Matters

Artificial Intelligence enhances care.

It never replaces therapists.

Clinical judgement always belongs to qualified professionals.

---

# The Ten Wellbeing Dimensions

The framework measures wellbeing across ten interconnected dimensions.

These dimensions collectively form a person's Living Wellbeing Profile.

---

## 1. Identity & Self-Awareness

Understanding:

- personal identity
- values
- beliefs
- strengths
- authenticity
- self-confidence

Questions explored include:

- Who am I?
- What matters to me?
- What makes me unique?

---

## 2. Life Story & Meaning

Understanding one's personal narrative.

Including:

- significant life events
- beliefs formed through experiences
- meaning making
- personal growth

Questions explored include:

- How has my story shaped me?
- What lessons have I learned?

---

## 3. Emotional Regulation

The ability to:

- identify emotions
- understand emotions
- express emotions
- regulate emotional responses

Questions explored include:

- How do I respond under pressure?
- How well do I understand my emotions?

---

## 4. Healing From The Past

Working through:

- trauma
- grief
- disappointment
- betrayal
- unresolved emotional wounds

Healing does not erase the past.

It transforms the relationship with it.

---

## 5. Resilience & Strength

Building the capacity to:

- adapt
- recover
- persevere
- maintain hope
- learn through adversity

---

## 6. Boundaries & Self-Protection

Developing healthy boundaries.

Including:

- emotional boundaries
- physical boundaries
- relational boundaries
- self-respect

---

## 7. Attachment & Relationships

Understanding:

- attachment styles
- trust
- communication
- relational patterns
- emotional safety

---

## 8. Love & Connection

Building meaningful relationships.

Including:

- intimacy
- empathy
- compassion
- belonging
- friendship
- family

---

## 9. Purpose & Future Self

Connecting everyday life with long-term purpose.

Including:

- goals
- aspirations
- contribution
- meaning
- vision

---

## 10. Wholeness & Self-Compassion

Learning to:

- accept oneself
- forgive oneself
- celebrate progress
- practice kindness
- embrace imperfection

# The Living Wellbeing Profile

Every client has a Living Wellbeing Profile.

The profile evolves continuously as new information becomes available.

It represents long-term wellbeing rather than isolated events.

The profile is informed by multiple sources.

---

# Intelligence Sources

The Living Wellbeing Profile combines insights from:

- Clinical Assessments
- MyDeepTalk Pillar Assessments
- Genesis Discoveries
- Journal Reflections
- Homework Progress
- Therapist Observations
- Session Outcomes

No single source defines the individual.

Instead, each source contributes evidence that strengthens the profile over time.

---

# Confidence Model

Each wellbeing dimension includes both a score and a confidence level.

Example:

Identity

Score: 78

Confidence: 28%

The score reflects current understanding.

Confidence reflects the amount and quality of evidence supporting that understanding.

As clients engage with MyDeepTalk, confidence increases naturally through additional observations and interactions.

---

# Wellbeing Intelligence

The MyDeepTalk Intelligence Layer continuously interprets information to identify:

- strengths
- growth opportunities
- emerging patterns
- progress over time
- therapist preparation insights
- recommended Genesis journeys
- recommended assessments
- journaling prompts

The Intelligence Layer never makes clinical diagnoses.

It provides contextual insights that support clients and therapists.

---

## Genesis

Updates the Living Wellbeing Profile.

---

---

Tracks progress.

---

# Product Design Principles

Every feature should answer:

- Which wellbeing dimension does this strengthen?
- Does it improve awareness?
- Does it encourage reflection?
- Does it support healing?
- Does it promote growth?
- Does it improve relationships?
- Does it contribute to the Living Wellbeing Profile?

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.

# Engineering Principles

The framework should remain:

- Modular
- Explainable
- Extensible
- Human-centred
- Privacy-conscious
- Clinically respectful

Business logic belongs within the Intelligence Layer.

User interfaces should present insights rather than implement business rules.

---

# Future Evolution

The Wellbeing Framework is intentionally designed to evolve.

Future intelligence sources may include:

- Wearable health data
- Mood tracking
- Voice sentiment analysis
- Community engagement
- Coaching experiences
- Learning pathways

All future capabilities should integrate through the Living Wellbeing Profile rather than creating independent wellbeing models.
